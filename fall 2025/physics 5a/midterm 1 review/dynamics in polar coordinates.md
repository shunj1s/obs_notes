uniform circular motion $\leftarrow$ radial acceleration due to the change in direction of motion

radial acceleration: $a_r = \ddot{r} - r\dot{\theta}^2$
only force on object is T towards the center, thus:
$$ -T = ma_r$$
$$ = m(\ddot{r} - r\dot{\theta}^2)$$
since $\dot{\theta} = v/R$, T = $\frac{mv^2}{R}$.

what if both radial and tangential motion?
$$ -(T + w\sin{\theta}) = ma_r$$
$$ = m(\ddot{r} - r\dot{\theta}^2)$$
$\theta + 2\alpha = \pi$ 
$\alpha = \frac{\pi - \theta}{2}$ 
$\beta = \frac{\pi}{2} - \alpha$
$\beta = \frac{\theta}{2}$
so, $\vec{F} = T\frac{\Delta L}{R} = m\frac{v^2}{R}$
$$T = \frac{m}{\Delta l}v^2 = \lambda v^2$$
